Mental Wellness Toolkit

Your mental health is a priority. Here are some simple strategies to help you manage stress and find calm amidst the pressure of student life.

Practice Mindfulness

Take 5 minutes each day to focus on the present moment. Notice the sights, sounds, and sensations around you without judgment. This can help reduce stress and improve focus.

Digital Detox

Schedule time away from your screens. Constant notifications and social media can be overwhelming. A short break can help clear your mind and reduce anxiety.

Stay Active

Physical activity is a powerful stress reliever. Even a short walk between study sessions can boost your mood and energy levels. Find an activity you enjoy and make it part of your routine.

Connect with Others

Don't hesitate to reach out to friends, family, or a counselor. Talking about what's on your mind can provide new perspectives and make you feel less alone.

Effective Study Habits

Studying smart is just as important as studying hard. Build a routine that supports both your learning and your wellbeing.

The Pomodoro Technique

Work in focused 25-minute intervals, separated by short 5-minute breaks. After four sessions, take a longer break. This helps maintain focus and prevents burnout.

Create a Study Sanctuary

Designate a clean, quiet, and organized space for studying. A dedicated area signals to your brain that it's time to focus, minimizing distractions.

Prioritize Sleep

Sleep is crucial for memory consolidation and learning. Aim for 7-9 hours per night to ensure your brain is rested and ready to absorb new information.

Find Professional Support

It's a sign of strength to ask for help. These organizations provide free, confidential support from trained professionals.

  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) - Education and support resources.
  • Crisis Text Line - Text HOME to 741741 for free, 24/7 crisis counseling.
  • BetterHelp - Online portal for professional therapy (note: subscription-based).
